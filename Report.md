# Development of Mechanized Weed Remover

## Abstract

Agriculture is one of the most important sectors of the economy, and weed management plays a critical role in improving crop productivity. Traditional methods of weed removal are labor-intensive, time-consuming, and physically demanding. To address these challenges, this project presents the development of a Mechanized Weed Remover that can efficiently remove weeds while reducing human effort and operational costs.

The machine is powered by a 12V rechargeable battery and utilizes a motor-driven hydraulic mechanism for weed extraction. The system is lightweight, portable, and designed to operate in different agricultural environments. The project aims to provide farmers with an affordable and environmentally friendly alternative to manual weeding.

---

## Introduction

Weeds compete with crops for nutrients, water, sunlight, and space, resulting in reduced crop yield and lower agricultural productivity. Farmers often rely on manual labor for weed removal, which requires significant effort and increases production costs.

The objective of this project is to design and develop a low-cost mechanized solution capable of removing weeds efficiently while minimizing human intervention. The machine combines mechanical, electrical, and hydraulic systems to achieve effective weed extraction.

## Working Principle

The machine operates using a rechargeable battery that supplies power to the DC motors and hydraulic system.

When activated:

1. Electrical energy from the battery powers the motors.
2. The hydraulic pump generates the necessary force.
3. The gas spring assists the lifting mechanism.
4. The weed-removal assembly uproots weeds from the soil.
5. Extracted weeds are separated from the cultivated crop area.

The machine reduces the physical effort required by farmers while maintaining effective weed-removal performance.
